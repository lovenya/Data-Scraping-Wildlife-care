WCS holiday toy drive continues through December 31



Bronx, N.Y. – Dec. 4, 2014 – Animals at WCS’s (Wildlife Conservation Society) Prospect Park Zoo and Queens Zoo will receive treats hidden inside holiday-themed enrichment items each weekend in December during the annual Presents to the Animals. 



*The schedule at the WCS Prospect Park Zoo is:

Weekends in December:

Dec. 6/7: dingoes-11 a.m.; Pallas’ cats -2:30 p.m. 

Dec. 13/14: North American river otters-11 a.m.; dwarf mongoose-2:30 p.m.

Dec. 20/21: Hamadryas baboons-11 a.m.; meerkats-2:30 p.m.

Dec. 27/28: Hamadryas baboons-11 a.m.; Geoffrey marmosets-2:30 p.m.



*The schedule at the WCS Queens Zoo is:

Weekends in December: 

Dec. 6/7, 13/14, 20/21: Puma-1 p.m.; mulefoot pigs-2 p.m.; Andean bears-3 p.m.



Continuing another holiday tradition, WCS is collecting toys at all five of its wildlife parks – the Bronx Zoo, Central Park Zoo, Prospect Park Zoo, Queens Zoo, and New York Aquarium. Toys collected will be donated to families in need within the communities they serve. Those donating an unwrapped toy at the Prospect Park Zoo and New York Aquarium will receive a ticket to the New York Aquarium; those donating at the Bronx Zoo, Central Park Zoo, or Queens Zoo will receive a ticket to the Bronx Zoo. Visit http://www.wcs.org/toydrive/ for more information.

Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS) – MISSION: WCS saves wildlife and wild places worldwide through science, conservation action, education, and inspiring people to value nature. VISION: WCS envisions a world where wildlife thrives in healthy lands and seas, valued by societies that embrace and benefit from the diversity and integrity of life on earth. To achieve our mission, WCS, based at the Bronx Zoo, harnesses the power of its Global Conservation Program in more than 60 nations and in all the world’s oceans and its five wildlife parks in New York City, visited by 4 million people annually. WCS combines its expertise in the field, zoos, and aquarium to achieve its conservation mission.
