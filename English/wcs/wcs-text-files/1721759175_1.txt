Bronx, NY
– April 4, 2011 – The
Bronx Zoo and New York Daily News have received just more than 33,000
nominations for a name of its Egyptian cobra.







Partnering
with the New York Daily News, the Bronx Zoo offered the public the opportunity
to submit their favorite name for the world-famous snake. Those
suggestions have been narrowed down to these five finalists, and the winner
will be chosen by public vote.



 Agnes - Greek for pure or holy; reference also
to St. AgnesAmaunet - A goddess in Ancient Egyptian
religion; meaning ‘the female hidden one’Cleopatra – The last pharaoh of Ancient Egypt;
killed herself by means of an Egyptian cobra biteSubira
– Egyptian for patientMia













Everyone is
encouraged to visit www.bronxzoo.com
to vote on their favorite. The winning name will be announced on
Thursday, April 7. Voting ends 12:01 a.m. EDT on Wednesday, April 6.
