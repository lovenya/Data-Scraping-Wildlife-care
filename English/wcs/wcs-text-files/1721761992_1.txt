New York State is the top importer of ivory into the United States, contributing to an industry that is leading to the extinction of African elephants. Despite the laws already in place, once ivory has made it into the country, it is difficult to regulate. 

John Calvelli, WCS Executive Vice President for Public Affairs and Director of the 96 Elephants Campaign, discusses proposed legislation to stop the sale of ivory in New York and why it’s so badly needed.
