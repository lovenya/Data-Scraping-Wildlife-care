The above image of a pangolin was captured by infrared camera s installed in the Wuqinzhang Community Reserve in Huidong County, Guangdong in summer 2020. Due to poaching and illegal trade, Chinese pangolins were assessed as “critically endangered” by the of Endangered Species in 2019 . According to CITES , from the 1960s to 2003, the population of pangolins in China dropped by more than 94%. The Chinese pangolin was even thought to be functionally extinct at the time of its assessment. S ince 2016, WildAid in partnership with other conservation organizations have been working on raising public awareness o f pangolin protection in China .
