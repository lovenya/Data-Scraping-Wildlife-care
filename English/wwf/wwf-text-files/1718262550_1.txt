In Africa, the warmest of welcomes The airstrip was barely identifiable in the middle of the khaki-toned desert. A stone building the size of a tollbooth stood in the blazing sun. A woman waited beside it, tucked under a triangular slice of shade. That woman, Lena Florry, was my key to understanding Namibia.
