In 2010, the world’s 13 tiger range countries made a landmark commitment to double the number of wild tigers by 2022. While some tiger populations have grown significantly since, with upward trends in India, Bhutan, Nepal, China, and Russia, these big cats are still widely threatened—and increasingly isolated—in Southeast Asia in particular.
