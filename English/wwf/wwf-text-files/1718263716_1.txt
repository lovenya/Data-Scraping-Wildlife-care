This past June, a tiny white object snaked back and forth through the skies over Montana. It was gathering thousands of images of prairie dog colonies, which are the main habitat of endangered black-footed ferrets. “People have been monitoring ferret habitat on foot here since 2013,” said Kristy Bly, a WWF biologist. “It’s a slow, painstaking process. This year, we put together a team to help us test whether a drone could do that work more quickly.” Learn more about how the research took place. Learn more about how the research took place.
