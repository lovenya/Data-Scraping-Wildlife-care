Zoonotic emerging infectious diseases are transmitted from a host animal, or reservoir species, to humans, sometimes by way of an intermediate host known as a vector. Diseases that can be spread from person to person (e.g., through respiratory droplets, like COVID-19) have the potential to become pandemics.
