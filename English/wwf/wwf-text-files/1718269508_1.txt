When Cyclone Nargis ripped across Myanmar’s southern coast in 2008, communities had little advance warning of the Category 4 storm, which unleashed widespread devastation and ultimately claimed 138,000 lives. Now, WWF-Myanmar has installed three automatic weather stations in climate-vulnerable areas of the country, providing communities a way to better predict, track, and plan for natural disasters.
