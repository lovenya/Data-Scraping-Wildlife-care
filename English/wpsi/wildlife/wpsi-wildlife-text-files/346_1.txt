Belinda Wright on state of India's wild spaces and the impact of lockdown

 

27 July 2020



A
two-time Emmy Award-winning wi­ldlife filmmaker, a firebrand activist
who risked it all to go undercover in one of the largest markets of
tiger parts in Tibet, and parent to an elephant that somehow labours
under the delusion that she’s actually a dog — Belinda Wright wears
many hats.

As I wait in my Zoom meeting room for her to log in,
I read about some of her exploits over the years — mapping the shadowy
world of animal trafficking and dealing with informers, tribal people
and the powers that be with equal aplomb.
