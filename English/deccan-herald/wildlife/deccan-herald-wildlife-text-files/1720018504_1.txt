The exhibition, which was inaugurated by Jairam Ramesh, Union Minister for Environment and Forests, has been put up by the Regional Museum of Natural History, Mysore, with funding from the Ministry of Environment and Forests.

According to the organisers, the exhibition has been put together as part of the International Year of Biodiversity (2010) and is being inaugurated in the International Year of Forests (2011). From the life of Todas, the ancient human inhabitants of the Western Ghats to information on the varied medicinal plants, the exhibition comprising 40 panels of 3x8 feet, features land, plants, animals and people of the Western Ghats. The exhibition, which will be open to public till Monday in Bangalore, will then be displayed in Mysore for visitors all through the monsoon. The exhibition will be later taken to Andhra Pradesh, Goa, Kerala, Lakshadweep and Puducherry.

The RMNH, which spent Rs 28 lakh on putting together the exhibition, has taken the assistance of Anthropological Survey of India, Mysore; ATREE, Bangalore; Botanical Survey of India, Coimbatore; M S Swaminathan Research Foundation, Wayanad; University of Agricultural Sciences, Bangalore; and Zoological Survey of India, Kozhikode.
