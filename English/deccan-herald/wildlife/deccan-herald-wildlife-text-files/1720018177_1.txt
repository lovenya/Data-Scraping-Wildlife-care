The single tusk measuring almost five feet (4.11 feet), weighs 17 kg and is valued at Rs 34 lakh in the international market of illegal wildlife trade. “The tusk has a diameter of one foot and three inches,” said Chikkasiddaiah, Sub Inspector of Chamarajnagar FMS. Chandranayaka, according to FMS Chamarajnagar, was in the trade since the days forest brigand Veerappan operated. “Several cases of sandalwood smuggling and illegal wildlife trade have been booked against him. He was arrested for possessing a tiger pelt in 2010 and the matter is pending before the local court,” said Chikkasiddaiah. An eight-month-old elephant calf died in a tiger attack in Veeranahosahalli range of Nagarhole National Park, reports DHNS from Hunsur. The tiger killed the calf while it was grazing near Basari Mara in CPT-5 block of Veeranahosahalli of the sanctuary. The incident came to the notice of the Forest department during regular patrolling by the watchers. The postmortem was conducted on the spot.
