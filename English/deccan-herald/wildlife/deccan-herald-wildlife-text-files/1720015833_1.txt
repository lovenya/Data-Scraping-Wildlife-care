For more than 30 years, she has been on TV, stage and film as an actor, director and producer and is currently working on scripts she wants to film. Ask her about her favourite travel destination and she says, “It has to be the Himalayas. In the last two years I have travelled the length and breath of Himachal — from the Miyar Valley in the top north west corner to the Sangla valley in the east, to Shimla, Mashobra, Shoja, and Manali, Naggar and across the Rohtang Pass, I just can’t seem to get enough of these spectacular mountains! I intend to go there at least twice every year from now on.” 

The mighty Himalayas are flocked by hordes of national and international tourists since years for its the sheer geographic diversity — from the forested valleys and tropical jungles of Arunachal boasting boasting of varied wildlife, to the rugged terrain of the mountains that extends to the Tibetan plateau. These mountains present an endless variation in terms of climate, geography, flora and fauna, and visiting its various destinations with rich cultural heritage of the region. For instance, the Leh and Ladakh region have become popular among many bikers and conventional tourists alike. Adding to this, are the regular destinations of Kullu-Manali, Sikkim, Darjeeling among others.ADVERTISEMENT
