New Delhi: The CBI has arrested a general manager of the National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) in an alleged bribery case of Rs 20 lakh in Maharashtra's Nagpur, officials said on Sunday.ADVERTISEMENTArvind Kale, who is also a project director, allegedly received the bribe from a private company, they said.The Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) recovered Rs 45 lakh cash, including the bribe of Rs 20 lakh, during a search operation following Kale's arrest, the officials said.A case has been registered against Kale and 11 others, including the private company, they said.
