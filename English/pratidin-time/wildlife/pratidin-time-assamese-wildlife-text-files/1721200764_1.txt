Meghalaya is home to several notable places for the conservation of wildlife, flora, and fauna. In this article, we will be looking at 5 National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries of Meghalaya. Nongkhyllem Wildlife Sanctuary is located in the state of Meghalaya, India. It was established in 1986 and covers an area of about 29 square kilometers. The sanctuary is home to several rare and endangered species, including the hoolock gibbon, capped langur, Asian elephant, and clouded leopard. The sanctuary is also known for its rich avifauna, with more than 50 species of birds recorded in the area. One of the major attractions of the sanctuary is the Nongkhyllem Cave, which is said to be one of the longest caves in Meghalaya. The cave is also home to a variety of fauna, including bats, swiftlets, and crickets. Visitors to the sanctuary can go on nature walks, bird watching, and trekking. The sanctuary also has a forest rest house for those who wish to spend the night in the wilderness. The best time to visit the sanctuary is between November and April when the weather is pleasant and the wildlife is more active. Nongkhyllem Wildlife Sanctuary is a must-visit for those who wish to experience the natural beauty and wildlife of Meghalaya. The Baghmara Reserve Forest is a vast and diverse forest reserve situated in the South Garo Hills district of Meghalaya, India. Encompassing an area of around 61 square kilometers, this reserve is a natural treasure trove, abundant with a wide range of flora and fauna. The forest is home to an extensive variety of wildlife, including tigers, elephants, bears, and several species of primates. Visitors can also experience the unique culture of the Garo tribe, one of the largest ethnic groups in Meghalaya, which resides in the surrounding areas. Trekking, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting are some of the exciting activities that visitors can enjoy in this reserve. Photography enthusiasts will find the Baghmara Reserve Forest a perfect location for capturing stunning and breath-taking scenic views. The forest is a must-see destination for nature lovers and anyone who wants to explore the mesmerizing beauty and rich cultural heritage of Meghalaya.
