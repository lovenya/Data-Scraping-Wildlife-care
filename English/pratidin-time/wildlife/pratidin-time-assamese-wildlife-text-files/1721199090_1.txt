The non-feature film, ‘Manah Aru Manuh’ (Manas and People) had bagged the Best Environment Film award at the prestigious 68th National Film Awards announced by the Directorate of Film Festivals on Friday. The documentary ‘Manah Aru Manuh’ narrates the story of how Manas is crucial for maintaining cultural significance of the indigenous Bodo and other communities. It tells human stories about the relationship between people and nature coded in cultural heritage, the rich biodiversity and the practices from multiple stakeholders to conserve the rich heritage.
