CHENNAI: In the early hours of Thursday, a fire broke out on the third floor of the Air Traffic Control (ATC) building at the airport in Chennai. According to officials, no damage was reported due to the incident.
 The Fire and Rescue Services officials suspect that the fire was caused by a short circuit in a room used for storing old items.
"Fire tenders from Ashok Nagar and Guindy were pressed into service and the fire was doused quickly, they said."
The officials confirmed that no one was harmed during the incident, and the airport's operations remained unaffected by the fire.
 The prompt response from the fire department ensured that the situation was brought under control swiftly, preventing any potential damage to the ATC building or disruption to the airport's functioning.
