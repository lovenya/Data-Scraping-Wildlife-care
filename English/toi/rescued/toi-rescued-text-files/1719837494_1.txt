PATIALA: A tragic incident unfolded on Thursday evening in Preet Colony, Ropar, where five labourers were trapped under the debris following the collapse of a residential building. The collapse was triggered by the sudden failure of an old concrete roof structure being elevated with construction instruments. The officials could not verify if the building was two storey or single-storey building however the neighbouring houses on both sides are two– storey buildings.
The district's swift response, led by Deputy Commissioner Ropar Preeti Yadav and SSP Gulneet Singh Khurana, saw the immediate mobilization of rescue teams from the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF), State Disaster Response Force (SDRF), and Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP).PollDo you think opposition's demand for SC-appointed probe into allegations of irregularities in NEET-UG 2024 exams justified?YesNoThe incident took place at around 4 PM.

By 7 pm, the relentless efforts of the rescue teams paid off as one labourer was extracted from the wreckage. Shortly after, a second labourer was also rescued. Both individuals were promptly sent to the Postgraduate Institute of Medical Education and Research (PGIMER) in Chandigarh for emergency medical treatment.

She assured that the rescue operations were being conducted with technical expertise and that efforts to rescue the remaining labourers were ongoing.
Additional Deputy Commissioner Puja Sial Garewal provided further insight into the incident, noting that the house, constructed in 1983, was undergoing a process to elevate its concrete roof by the labourers at the time of the collapse. She revealed that the occupants had temporarily relocated to a nearby property they owned. Garewal also stated that an extensive team of approximately 150 personnel, including 90 rescuers from NDRF, SDRF, and ITBP, were actively involved in the rescue operation, demonstrating a community united in the face of disaster.
The rescue operation remains ongoing as teams work tirelessly to locate and save the remaining trapped labourers. The incident has drawn attention to building safety standards and the importance of monitoring older structures undergoing renovation. Further updates are expected as the rescue operation progresses.
