NEW DELHI: Police in Navi Mumbai, Maharashtra, have rescued two child labourers from two food joints, an official said on Wednesday.
The children were found working at eateries in a vegetable market during a routine inspection. They were reportedly subjected to hard work, harassment, and were not paid properly.

The two juveniles were rescued and lodged in a remand home, he said.
A case was registered against the owners of the two eateries under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, the police added.
