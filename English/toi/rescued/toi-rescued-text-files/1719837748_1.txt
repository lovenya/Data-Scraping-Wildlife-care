BALLIA: A 16-year-old girl who had been missing for over two months was rescued by the police in Ballia, Uttar Pradesh. The officials also arrested a 23-year-old man named Nirbhay Sahni, who allegedly abducted and repeatedly raped the minor. Sahni was taken into custody on Monday and sent to jail.

Additional Superintendent of Police Durga Prasad Tiwari stated that the police located the girl at the Ballia railway station on Sunday and apprehended Sahni the following day. In her statement to the police, the victim alleged that Sahni had raped her for nearly two months after abducting her.
Based on the victim's statement, "Section 376 (3) (rape of girl below 16 years of age) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) and the relevant section of the POCSO Act" were added to the case. Sahni was arrested at the Ballia railway station on Monday and subsequently sent to jail.
