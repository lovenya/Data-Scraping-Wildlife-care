Lucknow: The relentless crackdown on illegal drug trafficking by state government’s Anti-Narcotics Task Force (ANTF) dealt a severe blow to drug dealers across the state, making 293 arrests in recent operations.
In a span of two and a half years, the ANTF has seized 11,992.99 kg drugs worth over Rs 91 crore. The intensified campaign, initiated under the direct supervision of Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath, aims to stem flow of narcotics endangering the health and lives of UP youth, a home officer added.

Subsequent operations in 2023 led to the arrest of 252 drug dealers, with seizures totaling 10,369.14 kg drugs valued at Rs 73.85 crore.
In the current year, ANTF operations have already resulted in the arrest of 31 individuals and seizure of 1,594.97 kg narcotics, valued at Rs 14.63 crore. Notably, significant quantities of morphine, heroin, charas, opium, and ganja have been confiscated, along with cash, vehicles, and other assets from the accused.
