NEW DELHI: The Supreme Court on Monday said economic offences should not be equated with crimes like drug trafficking, murder, rape, dacoity etc to deny bail and held that an accused can be granted bail on the ground of delay in trial and his long incarceration.
While rejecting the bail plea of senior AAP leader Manish Sisodia, a bench of Justices Sanjeev Khanna and SVN Bhatti said speedy trial was a fundamental right of an accused and it was a wrong perception that an accused should not be granted bail under a Prevention of Money Laundering Act case till he had suffered incarceration for the specified period.
