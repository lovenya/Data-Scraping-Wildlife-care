CHENNAI: The National Investigation Agency (NIA) on Wednesday arrested two men in Tamil Nadu and one in Puducherry after carrying out searches in six places in connection with the cases of trafficking Bangladesh and Myanmar nationals to meet the demand for workforce and employing them with the fake Aadhaar cards as if they were Indian nationals.

Across the country, the searches were carried out at 55 locations in 10 states and Union territories including Tripura, Assam, West Bengal, Karnataka, Telangana, Haryana, Rajasthan, Jammu and Kashmir, along with the Border Security Force (BSF) and state police forces.
In Tamil Nadu, searches were carried out in Pallikaranai, Perumbakkam, Padappai, Tiruvallur, Coimbatore and Tirupur. "The operation was aimed at dismantling illegal human trafficking support networks involved in infiltration and settlement of illegal migrants across the Indo-Bangladesh border into India," said NIA.
