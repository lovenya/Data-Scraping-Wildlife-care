Chandigarh: Lesser percentage of missing children were traced in 2022 and 2023 as compared to 2020 and 2021.
In 2020, 95% of the missing children were traced. In the next year, the traced children percentage rose to 98%, but dropped to 88% in 2022 and 68% for 2023 till now.

Meanwhile, a committee was constituted along with directions to ensure speedy formulation of intervention plan for traced and missing children and to further analyse the cases in order to identify the factors which led to abduction of children or their decision to run away from home.
The intervention plan formulated by the committee consists of monthly financial assistance of Rs 4,000 under sponsorship scheme of the ministry of women and child development, institutional care services if required, counselling services and enrollment vocational programmes being run by the department of social welfare in Vocational Training Centre, Asha Kiran, Sector 46, Chandigarh.
