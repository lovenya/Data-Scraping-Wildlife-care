CHENNAI: About three weeks after the National Investigation Agency (NIA) cracked down on human trafficking and searched 55 locations spread over 10 states, a city court has sent three arrested people, including two Bangladeshis, to a week-long NIA custody.
Prime suspect Shahabudeen, who was arrested from Padappai, Babu S K, who was secured from Puducherry, and Munna alias Noor Karim, arrested from Chengalpet, were handed over to NIA on Thursday for custodial interrogation.They will be produced before the court on November 30.
NIA sources said that while Babu is from the border area of West Bengal, the other two are Bangladeshi nationals. While all three spoke only in Bengali after the arrest, inquiry revealed that they can speak in Tamil fluently.
NIA sources said that all three accused had at least 100 to 150 people under their control and sent them to different workplaces on demand, providing them with shelter and food.

On November 8, NIA busted the illegal human trafficking network and infiltration of Bangladeshi men in the guise of Indian nationals after carrying out searches at 55 locations in 10 states and union territories, including Tripura, Assam, West Bengal, Karnataka, Telangana, Haryana, Rajasthan, Jammu and Kashmir. The raids were carried out along with the Border Security Force (BSF) and state police forces.
In Tamil Nadu, the searches were done at Pallikaranai, Perumbakkam and Padappai in Chennai, besides in Tiruvallur, Coimbatore and Tirupur districts.
NIA also seized mobile phones, SIM cards and pen drives, besides identity-related documents, including fake Aadhaar cards and PAN cards, 20 lakh in cash and $4,550.
