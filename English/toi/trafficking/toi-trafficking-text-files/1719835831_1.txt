Chandigarh: Haryana has invoked the Prevention of Illicit Trafficking in Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (PITNDPS) Act of 1988 to clamp down on the big-fish drug operators, dealers, financiers, and kingpins who work behind the scenes. Police have home department’s approval for detaining nine mega drug traffickers, eight of whom have already been sent to various jails in Haryana.

Once a preventive detention order is issued under the PITNDPS Act, those accused can be held for at least a year, while even their relatives and associates will lose their illegal properties under the NDPS Act of 1985.
These eight detenues are from Sonipat, Mahendragarh, Panipat, Rewari, Gurugram, Panchkula, and Kaithal districts, while the ninth whose detention is approved is Iqbal ‘Kranti’ of Pinangwa’s Gujarwada Mohalla in Nuh district. He is alleged to be a commercial trafficker of ganja with two FIRs for trafficking 113 and 38 kilograms of the drug in separate cases. His wife was also booked in 1985.
