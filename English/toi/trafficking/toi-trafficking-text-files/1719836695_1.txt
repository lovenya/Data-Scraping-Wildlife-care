Hyderabad: Telangana has witnessed a significant surge in child trafficking cases, surpassing the pre-Covid figures of 2016-2020 by almost three times, according to a report released on Sunday on the occasion of World Day Against Trafficking in Persons.

Uttar Pradesh, Bihar and Andhra Pradesh are the top three states with the maximum number of children trafficked between 2016 and 2022, while Delhi has seen an alarming 68% rise from pre to post-Covid times, according to the study.
It has also recorded the plight of child workers in various industries across the country. The sectors employing the maximum number of children were hotels and ‘dhabas,’ followed by ‘mom & pop’ automobile or transport industry, and garments.
