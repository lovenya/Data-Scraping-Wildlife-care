Noida: In an effort to contain increasing incidents of poaching and other crimes against wild animals, the forest and wildlife department of UP is planning to set up a crime control ‘bureau’ in Noida. 
H V Girish, officiating district forest officer of Gautam Budh Nagar, has been appointed as the nodal officer of the bureau. However, an office building for it is yet to be identified.
“I will commence work on setting up the bureau as soon as I get released from the post of district forest officer, Gautam Budh Nagar, where I am officiating. As soon as the next officer joins, I will start working on my new assignment,” H V Girish told TOI.
Uttar Pradesh has been under the scanner of the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) for recording the highest wildlife crime in India. Reports updated till 2016 shows UP topping the list in wildlife crime, followed by Rajasthan.
Following reports of the NCRB released earlier this year, 852 wildlife crime cases were reported in the country in 2016 of which 302 emerged from Uttar Pradesh followed by 190 in Rajasthan. Number of species vulnerable to poaching and crime were recorded at 400 in 2014 and went up to 465 in 2016. 
In 2016, 50 tigers were poached in the country, the highest in the past decade. Apart from this, 340 peacocks were killed by poachers between 2015 and 2016, which is 193% higher than that of 2014. The most vulnerable animals in the country are the blackbuck, elephant, leopard, rhinoceros, chinkara, blue bull, spotted deer and the wild boar.
