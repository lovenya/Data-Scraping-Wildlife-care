INDORE: A year after notification of Indore’s chief judicial magistrate court for trial of major wildlife crime related cases of 16 districts, seven highly sensitive cases of illegal trade have been registered here.
Of the seven cases being tried here, chargesheet has been produced in six while one case has recently been registered against a man trading rare coral reefs and exotic turtles.

Among the cases which are being tried before CJM Keshav Singh here are those in which the accused was found trading exotic snakes and tortoises, leopard skin, jackal nails etc. Among the accused are highly qualified and literate persons, trading the exotic wildlife items through use of e-commerce websites. Twenty one people have been made accused in the seven cases including four women. Of these 21, four are absconding.

An application is pending before the state government for the appointment of five lawyers at Indore, Sagar, Satna, Hoshangabad and Jabalpur where the five regional offices of tiger strike force have been set up,” additional principal chief conservator of forests Alok Kumar said. 
To spread awareness among the judiciary regarding wildlife crime cases, a special workshop was also held last year wherein participating ADPOs and magistrates were told about the various acts related to wildlife and the illegal trade of such products.
