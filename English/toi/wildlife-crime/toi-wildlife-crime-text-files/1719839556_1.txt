NEW DELHI: Officials of Directorate of Revenue Intelligence (DRI) on Thursday seized two leopard skins from four people at Azadpur Mandi. The accused have been detained and handed over to the forest and wildlife department.

"Continuing its endeavour for safeguarding the country's fauna, DRI detained four people in Delhi under Section 50(1)(c) of The Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972 and recovered two leopard skins from their custody. The four detained people were handed over to forest officials for arrest and further investigation," said a DRI official.
No response was received from the forest department.
