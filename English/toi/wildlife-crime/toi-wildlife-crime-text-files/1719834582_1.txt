MUMBAI: Several protected bird species and also bats were rescued from a house in Fort in south Mumbai during a joint operation by Wildlife Crime Control Bureau and the state forest department late on Tuesday night.
The seized species were 110 kites, 43 parrots, eight bats, seven barn owls, an eagle owl, two night herons and a seagull. Officials said that the birds and bats were kept in cages on the terrace of the house by the resident, Pradeep Gabriel D’Souza.
Range forest officer Rakesh Bhoir said: “Since all these birds are scheduled species under Wildlife Act, a preliminary offence report has been lodged against for illegally keeping the birds and bats.”
