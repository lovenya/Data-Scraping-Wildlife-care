THANE: The police on Friday nabbed two persons from Maharashtra's Thane district for carrying ambergris worth Rs 4.30 crore.
The duo was held near Thane's Ghodbunder Road.
According to Thane police, officials had received a tip-off about the same, post which a Crime Branch Unit V team was deployed to nab the culprits.
On Thursday evening, they intercepted a two-wheeler at an old octroi post in Ghodbunder and recovered the ambergris from the duo.

A case under Wildlife Protection Act has been lodged and Kasarwadavli police is probing further," a police official informed.
This is the third time in the month of June that ambergris has been seized from Thane and Mumbai regions. Earlier, the banned substance worth Rs 2.7 crore and Rs 7 crore was recovered from Mumbai in two separate incidents.
Ambergris, or commonly known as whale vomit, is found in the intestine of the sperm whale. It is listed in Schedule 1 of the Wildlife Protection Act and is highly priced as a prime ingredient in high-end perfumery and the manufacture of medicines.
