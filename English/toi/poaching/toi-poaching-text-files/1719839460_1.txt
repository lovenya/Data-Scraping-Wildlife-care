RAJKOT: The wildlife circle of Gujarat forest department, in a joint operation with the Indian coast guard, busted a racket in dolphin poaching off Porbandar coast and arrested ten fishermen with 22 dead dolphins on Wednesday evening.

The dolphins are found on the coast of Porbandar, Dwarka, Okha and Jamnagar.

According to the forest department, the ten accused are natives of Tamil Nadu, Orissa, Asam, Kerala and other states. 
The accused were identified as Sansuman Basumatri (21), Assam); Nihal Kunanchery (26), Kerala; Giltus Muppakudi (62), Kerala; Selvan Surlesh (46), Tamil Nadu; Rajkumar Tanisharaj (52), Tamil Nadu; Aarun Pillai (47), Tamil Nadu; Anthony Barla (50), Tamil Nadu; Mayadhar Raut (37), Odisha; Ranjit Boro (28), Assam; and Saujin Susayarul (36), Tamil Nadu.

The coast guard, who had specific information of poaching of marine mammals, informed the forest department and carried out a joint operation off Porbandar coast. 
They found a suspicious boat 12 nautical miles off Porbandar coast and found the dead dolphins. 
Talking to TOI, Aaradhna Sahu, chief conservator of forest wildlife circle, said "We have sent the carcasses of dolphins for the penal postmortem and arrested 10 fishermen and booked them under the relevant sections of Wildlife Protection Act for poaching protected marine mammals." 
The forest department is probing the trafficking of this protected species.
