RAMANATHAPURAM: The forest department has arrested a 30-year-old man and is on the lookout for his 25-year-old brother for poaching a dugong and cutting it into pieces to sell its meat at Thondi in Ramanathapuram district of Tamil Nadu on Friday. 
“We seized 300 kg of meat of the female that is protected under Schedule I of The Wildlife Protection Act, 1972,” Ramanathapuram forest range officer B Jabez told TOI.
The officer said that the animal would have weighed more than 400 kg and that it was to be sold for Rs 600 to Rs 800 per kg. Inquiry by the department personnel revealed that the docile animal was caught using net from the boat by V Rakappan, 30, of Nambuthalai and his brother V Palani, 25, around 12.45 am on Friday.
The brothers brought it to shore around 2am and they cut to pieces with the help of a couple of people and kept the meat in a thermocol box for sale in a fish stall on the Dharga Street in MGR Nagar, Thondi.
