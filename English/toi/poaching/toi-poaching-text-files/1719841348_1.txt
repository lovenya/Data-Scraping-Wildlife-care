RAIPUR: The Judicial Magistrate in Gariyaband has placed a man on a 15-day remand for hunting bear and wild pig in the Udanti-Sunabeda Wildlife Sanctuary on the Odisha border. Previously, the Anti-Poaching team had apprehended the accused.
According to Varun Jain, deputy director of Udanti Sitanadi Tiger Reserve, Lakhmu Sori, a 39-year-old resident of Amamora village in the Kulhadighat forest range, hunted two wild pigs in the Sunabeda Wildlife Sanctuary for a family feast a year ago and kept four teeth of the animal in his house.PollDo you think opposition's demand for SC-appointed probe into allegations of irregularities in NEET-UG 2024 exams justified?YesNoHe used a bow and arrow to hunt the pigs. Additionally, he cut off the paw of a dead bear in the jungle of Odisha and kept it in his house.
The accused was looking for a buyer to sell the animal parts. The forest team received a tip-off and arrested him on May 3 with the help of assistant ranger Baikunth Singh Thakur.
The forest team arrested the accused under the relevant sections of the Wildlife (Conservation) Act 1972 and presented him in the court of the judicial magistrate in Gariyaband, as stated by the deputy director.
