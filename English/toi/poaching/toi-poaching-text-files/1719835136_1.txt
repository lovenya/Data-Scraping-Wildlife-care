BHOPAL/JABALPUR: In a major breakthrough, the Wildlife Crime Control Bureau (WCCB) and authorities of the Bandhavgarh Tiger Reserve have busted a gang of poachers. 
The gang targeted monitor lizards, deer, pangolins and wild boars in the forest close to the protected areas.
Skins of six monitor lizards, 24 deer horns and pangolin scales were seized from their possession. "They target lizards during monsoon, when it comes out of rock crevices or burrows. They become victims while basking in the sunlight. Poachers make use of iron rods or just dogs to sniff them out. They are caught by the neck and strangled," said the official.
