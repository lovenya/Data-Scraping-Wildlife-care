Villupuram: A 17-year-old boy from Andhra Pradesh was arrested by forest officials in Villupuram district on charges of poaching 33 Indian flap shell turtles from a waterbody near Valavanur. The Indian flap shell turtle comes under Schedule 1 (part II) of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972. The boy is the son of a nomadic tribal couple, who are fortune tellers from AP.

He was produced before the juvenile justice board, which sent him to an observation home. The forest officials later released the turtles in a river near Kandachipuram in the district.
Police said five tribal families from AP, leading a nomadic life and eking out a living by fortune-telling with their decorated boom boom oxen, reached Villupuram district a few days ago. On Monday, four minor boys of the group reached a water body near Valavanur. One of them poached 33 freshwater turtles. He was booked under the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972.tnn
