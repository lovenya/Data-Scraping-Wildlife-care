HYDERABAD: Forest officials have seized 3810 traps and snares that were laid to catch, poach or kill wildlife in forest areas in different parts of the state.
In the ‘Catch the trap’ initiative introduced from December 1, 2023, the traps and snares were seized. In all 4,872 kg of material used for the purpose was seized. Chief Wildlife Warden and Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife), M C Pargaien who had launched the initiative inspected the material that was seized which was transported to the Mahavir Harina Vanasthali National Park in the city for proper documentation on Wednesday.
“The main purpose of the transportation of the seized material is to ensure that the material is not reused or recycle and to take remedial measures and formulate strategies for the future to protect wildlife,” he said.
Since the ‘Catch the trap’ initiative was launched six months ago, till date only three cases of electrocution of animals were reported as the snares were removed. In 2021, there were 18 cases, in 2022 there were 15 cases and in 2023, there were 21 cases.

While officials have advised villagers against doing that, it has resulted in cases in which humans got electrocuted.
For example in January 2023, a 45-year-old man Gugulath Mangya Naik died when he came into contact with an live electric wire arranged by hunters to trap wild animals in Rudrangie mandal of Rajanna Sircilla district. Similarly, a 45-year-old man Rajanna died in Mancherial in April 2023, another person, D Sudhakar got electrocuted in Khammam in December 2023.
In another instance, a 40-year-old man, Kotnaik Bheemrao got electrocuted in Komarambheem Asifabad district, in January 2024. A Greyhounds comnanda, A Praveen got electrocuted during a combing operation in Jayashankar Bhoopalpalli district in Feb 2024 and in the same month, a 60-year-old man Pendore Laxamn died in Adilabad when he came into contact with a live wire.
From April 1, 2023 to March 31, 2024, the number of wild animals which died due to natural reasons, accidentally or hunted were two tigers, three leopards, two sloth bears, two gaur/Indian Bison and 143 other herbivores and other mammals.
