The Wildlife Photographer of the Year 2023 competition has unveiled its winning images which include mushroom, a beached orca taking its final breaths, and a pair of Nubian ibex battling on a clifftop, as reported by CNN.
This year's title was awarded to Laurent Ballesta, a French underwater photographer and marine biologist.

The Wildlife Photographer of the Year competition, now in its 59th year, is organized by the Natural History Museum of London.
The winners were chosen from a pool of nearly 50,000 entries representing 95 countries. The announcement of the winners took place at an awards ceremony on Tuesday.
These images will be on display at the museum starting this Friday.
"Whilst inspiring absolute awe and wonder, this year’s winning images present compelling evidence of our impact on nature – both positive and negative,” said Doug Gurr, director of the Natural History Museum, in a statement.
“Global promises must shift to action to turn the tide on nature’s decline,” the report quoted him as adding.
