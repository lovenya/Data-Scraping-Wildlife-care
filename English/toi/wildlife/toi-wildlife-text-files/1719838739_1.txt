CHENNAI: Customs officers at Chennai airport on Saturday seized around 5,000 red-eared slider turtle hatchlings that were abandoned at the international arrival terminal. They launched a hunt for the flyer who smuggled the hatchlings from Malaysia.
Red-eared turtles of Mexican origin are in-demand exotic species in the US to be reared as pets. Two suitcases that were part of the checked-in luggage that arrived in a Batik Air flight on Friday night were not claimed by any passenger. On being informed, customs officers and CISF personnel checked the baggage with metal detectors for suspicious articles, after which they were opened in a safe manner. 
The suitcases had several plastic boxes inside with holes and were full of red-eared slider turtle hatchlings.

This is the second wildlife seizure at Chennai airport within a week. On April 12, customs officers seized 484 red-eared slider turtles and nine African spurred tortoises from a flyer identified as Mohamed Mubin. Based on his inputs, a suspended cop was arrested. 
In 2023-24, customs sleuths thwarted 17 bids of smuggling exotic pets into the country and seized a variety of species including monkeys, rats and snakes.
