Kolhapur: The state government has declared a new conservation reserve in the forest land of Sangli district — Atpadi conservation reserve — connecting the Maini conservation area in the west with the Maldhok bird sanctuary in the northeast.

The Atpadi conservation reserve is home to 36 species of trees, 116 species of herbs, 15 species of shrubs, 14 species of vine and one parasitic plant.
Honorary wildlife warden for Sangli, Ajitkumar Patil, told TOI that the efforts of honorary wildlife warden for Sangli district, Late Papa Patil and the additional principal chief conservator of forests, Western Wildlife Division, Mumbai, Clement Ben, have paid off. “Patil and Ben made great efforts to ensure the protection of the area,” Patil said.
