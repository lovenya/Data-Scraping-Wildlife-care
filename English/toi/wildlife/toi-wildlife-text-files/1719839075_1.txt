GANDERBAL/SRINAGAR: A wildlife department official bravely confronted a leopard who was wandering in a village in central Kashmir's Ganderbal district on Wednesday. Using only a stick, the official fought the leopard, which jumped on him, and then wrestled it into submission with his bare hands.

He maintained a safe distance from the leopard until he grabbed a stick to try and control it. But then, the leopard attacked him, biting his hand. Soon, locals including wildlife officials, quickly came to help and hit the leopard with wood and sticks.
Ultimately, the man managed to free his hand from the leopard's hold and calm it down. With the help of locals, they captured the leopard alive and tranquilized the wild cat.
