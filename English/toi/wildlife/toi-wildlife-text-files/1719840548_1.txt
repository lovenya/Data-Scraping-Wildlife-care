Margao: The state forest department has intensified its drive against poaching of frogs during the monsoon.
On Saturday two persons were booked by the department on charges of poaching frogs in the fields at Quitol, Betul, under provisions of the Wildlife Protection Act. 
Forest department officials said that the indiscriminate killing of frogs for their meat has led to a drastic decline in the frog population in the state.Catching, killing and selling frogs, or serving frog meat at eating places contravenes the provisions of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972.
Goacan, an NGO, has also launched a campaign to raise awareness among the public against poaching of frogs. TNN
