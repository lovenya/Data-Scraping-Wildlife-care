Bhubaneswar: Two days after two elephant tusks were seized from Baisipalli wildlife sanctuary in Nayagarh district, a joint team of forest and directorate of revenue intelligence (DRI) on Saturday raided a jewellery shop in Deogarh and seized the tusks, weighing 4.2 kg and 4.7 kg.

“We are trying to explore the links of the accused by verifying their call records. It will help us ascertain if they tried to sell the tusks outside through wildlife traders,” said divisional forest officer (Deogarh), Magar Dhanaji Raoso.
The accused were identified as shop owner Premraj Sahu (65), Rajesh Kumar (49), middleman from Haryana, and Samir Kapardar (49). Rajesh has criminal antecedents, especially in wildlife crimes, sources said.
