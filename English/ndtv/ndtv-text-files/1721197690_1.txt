He and the snake did not make the flight that was headed to Barbados, authorities said.The incident unfolded Sunday at a screening area of the Miami International Airport. An article in the Miami Herald said Transportation Security Administration officers found the snake inside a piece of checked luggage when a TSA security officer noticed something odd.Officials did not tell the Herald how big the snake was, why the man said he had the snake and what airline was involved.Sari Koshetz, a spokeswoman for the TSA, told the newspaper that a bomb expert then examined the bag and found the python - still alive - in the hard drive.In a Twitter message, Koshetz wrote that the snake was "artfully concealed" inside the hard drive."This organic mass was not explosive," she wrote, "but it shows you can't hide any threat from us."The snake, Koshetz told the paper, was "obviously not an imminent terrorist threat." Koshetz told the Herald that the snake was a "possible wildlife threat" on a plane.
