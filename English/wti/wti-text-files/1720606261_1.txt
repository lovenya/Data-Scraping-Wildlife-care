It is with profound sadness that the Wildlife Trust of India (WTI) announces the passing of Dr. A.J.T. Johnsingh, a luminary in the field of conservation, who breathed his last today, at 78 years old.
Dr. A.J.T. Johnsingh is a luminary whose life story is interwoven with the very fabric of India’s wilderness. With unwavering dedication and boundless passion, Dr. Johnsingh has left an indelible mark on the landscape of conservation, inspiring countless individuals and organizations to protect our precious natural heritage.
 

Throughout his illustrious career, Dr. Johnsingh emerged as a stalwart champion of conservation, spearheading numerous initiatives to protect India’s biodiversity. From conducting pioneering research on endangered species to advocating for the establishment of protected areas and identifying India’s elephant corridors, his contributions have been instrumental in shaping the conservation landscape of the country. His efforts have not only safeguarded countless species from extinction but have also empowered local communities to become stewards of their natural heritage.
His legacy is not measured merely in accolades and achievements but in the countless lives he has touched and the enduring impact of his work, including several of our colleagues at Wildlife Trust of India. His passion, perseverance, and unwavering commitment serve as a beacon of hope for future generations of conservationists, inspiring them to follow in his footsteps and protect the precious biodiversity of our planet.
