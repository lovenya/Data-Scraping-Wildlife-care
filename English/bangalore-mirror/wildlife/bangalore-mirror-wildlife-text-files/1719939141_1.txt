Wildlife lovers are mourning the death of Bhogeshwara, the 70-year-old tusker with the longest tusks among Asian elephants.
 
Bhogeswara, the majestic elephant, was found dead near the Kabini reservoir in Bandipur-Nagarhole Reserve Forest on June 11. He was known as Mr Kabini.
 
The elephant was a cynosure of all eyes tourists as it was blessed with the longest tusks in the continent.
 
The Forest Department is mulling to obtain permission to preserve its tusks at the exhibition centre.
 
One of his tusks was 2.54 metre (8 feet) long and the other measured 2.34 metre (7.5 feet). Both the tusks almost touched the ground.
