Kolkata: Flood situation in Assam remained critical on Wednesday as river Brahmaputra and its tributaries flowed above the danger level in several districts of the state. 

Assam Health Minister Himant Biswa Sarma said that the death toll has reached 20.

Meanwhile, animal deaths have also increased. According to latest figures provided by the Assam governmentâ€™s Eastern Assam Wildlife Division, the death toll of animals in Kaziranga National Park and Tiger Reserve stands at 24, up from 15.

Death toll of endangered one-horned Rhinos touched four on Wednesday. Among other animals, 14 hog deer, one
elephant, four wild boars and two Sambhar also lost their lives to the natural disaster. The forest department has rescued about 57 animals so far from the national park. 

According to Assam State Disaster Management Authority (ASDMA), more than 57 lakh people have been affected in around 4,626 villages in Assam. Around 1,51,947 people have been shifted to 819 relief camps. However, ASDMA also mentioned that water receeded in the Kaziranga National Park by 50cm.

The National Disaster Response Force (NDRF), Army and State Disaster Response Force (SDRF) have been pressed into action and are working to rescue the flood affected people. Forest and police personnel are also maintaining a strict vigil to ensure safety of animals. Traffic on the national highways have also been restricted.

In Assam, many districts including Kokrajhar, Dhubri, Morigaon, Goalpara are affected due to flood and vast stretches of Kaziranga, Manas National Parks and Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary are inundated.
