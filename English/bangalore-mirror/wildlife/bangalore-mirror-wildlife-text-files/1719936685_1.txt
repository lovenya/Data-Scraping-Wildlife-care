Two bears - a male and a female - died after biting on crude explosives kept by villagers to keep wild boars away from their fields.
 
 Speaking to Bangalore Mirror, Venkatesh G Assistant Conservator of Forests, Kanakapura said the incident was reported from Thamasandra village which is about 2-3 kilometers from the Bananthamari forest range. It is learnt that the villagers had placed 2-3 crude bombs to protect their fields from wild boars. Unfortunately, the bears that came to the field became the victims of the explosives. They were aged about eight years, he said. A complaint has been lodged under section 216, 35,9,39, 50 and 51 of the Wildlife Protection Act. The case is under investigation. The presence of bears is noticed in villages across Ramnagar district.
